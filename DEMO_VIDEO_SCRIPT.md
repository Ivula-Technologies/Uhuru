# Three-minute demo video outline

Use your own voice and make the wording sound natural to you.

## 0:00–0:20 — Introduce the project

“Hi, I’m [your name]. This is *Uhuru: Eyes of the Land*, a narrative educational adventure about Kenyan history. I wanted to begin with ordinary people and community life, not only major political figures.”

Show the title screen.

## 0:20–0:55 — Play the village

“This prototype is the Prologue, before colonial rule. The player explores a village and learns through conversations with an elder, farmer, trader, weaver and water carrier.”

Move through the village and briefly show two NPCs.

## 0:55–1:25 — Show accessibility and learning

“When a conversation opens, the world pauses, so players can read at their own pace. Conversations unlock journal entries and connect farming, trade, craft and water stewardship to the historical context.”

Show dialogue, then journal and quest log.

## 1:25–1:55 — Show systems

“The vertical slice includes movement, choices, quests, collectibles, inventory, journal, map, time of day and local saving.”

Show an interaction, inventory or map, then press F5 to save.

## 1:55–2:35 — Codex and GPT-5.6

“I built this with Codex Desktop and GPT-5.6 as my coding collaborator. I used it to turn the design documents into Godot scenes and GDScript systems, organise the supplied asset concepts, and refine the project after visual testing. For example, feedback about clipped fixed UI and slow reading led to responsive UI anchors and world-pausing dialogue.”

## 2:35–3:00 — Close

“This is a focused prototype, not a finished history of Kenya. My next step is to develop the Prologue and Chapter 1 with original production assets and continuing historical consultation. Thank you for watching.”

End on the title screen and repository URL.
